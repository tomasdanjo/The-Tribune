Uphold Journalistic Integrity

  • Accuracy

    AI-generated content should be fact-checked rigorously before publication to ensure it meets The Tribune’s standards for accuracy and reliability.

  • Sources

    Ensure that AI-generated articles or insights are supported by verified and credible sources. AI should never fabricate or speculate information.

Transparency and Disclosure

  • Clear Labeling

    Any content produced by AI should be clearly labeled as such, so readers can distinguish between human-written and AI-assisted articles.

  • Methodology Disclosure

    When AI tools are used for data analysis or content creation, the methodology and type of AI used should be disclosed to maintain trust.

Fairness and Bias Minimization

  • Bias Audits

    AI models must be regularly audited to identify and minimize any biases. These audits should be conducted by diverse teams to ensure impartiality.

  • Balanced Representation

    AI-generated content should strive to represent multiple perspectives and avoid reinforcing stereotypes or promoting biased narratives.

Privacy and Data Ethics

  • User Privacy

    AI tools must adhere to stringent data privacy laws and best practices to protect user information.

  • Ethical Data Use

    Ensure that data used to train AI models is sourced ethically and, where applicable, anonymized to prevent misuse.

Human Oversight

  • Editorial Review

    AI-generated articles must go through human editorial review before publication. Editors should assess the content for clarity, factual correctness, and adherence to The Tribune’s editorial standards.

  • Collaboration

    AI should be seen as an assistant to human journalists, helping them produce content faster, but not as a replacement for human insight and oversight.

Engagement and Interactivity

  • User Interaction

    AI should be used to facilitate meaningful engagement with readers, such as responding to inquiries, providing recommendations, and personalizing content in a way that maintains the organization's tone and values.

  • Feedback Loop

    Collect reader feedback on AI-generated content and use it to improve the algorithms and refine future outputs.

Ethical Advertising

  • Non-Invasive

    Use AI in a way that does not compromise user trust through intrusive advertising or content placement.

  • Ad Transparency

    Ensure that any AI-driven ad placement is disclosed to readers so that they are aware of when content is sponsored or personalized.

Security and Safety

  • Data Security

    Protect the AI systems and data they use against unauthorized access, data breaches, and potential misuse.

  • Cybersecurity Measures

    Regularly update AI tools and systems to protect against vulnerabilities.

AI Limitations Awareness

  • Knowledge Boundaries

    AI should be used for tasks that align with its capabilities. Do not rely on AI for tasks it cannot accurately handle, such as investigative journalism that requires human intuition and critical thinking.

  • Quality Control

    Ensure that AI-generated content is continually monitored and updated to prevent outdated or incorrect information from being disseminated.

Commitment to Human-Centric AI

  • Support for Journalists

    AI should be used as a tool that enhances journalists' work, rather than replacing them. Training for staff on how to work effectively with AI should be provided.

  • Diversity and Inclusion

    AI technologies should be designed and implemented with inclusivity in mind, considering diverse viewpoints and ensuring that underrepresented voices are reflected and respected.

These guidelines aim to foster the responsible use of AI at The Tribune while maintaining high journalistic standards and trust with readers.
